Verbal Aspect - Complete Explanation

Verbal aspect (вид глагола) is a fundamental concept in Russian. There are two aspects: imperfective (несовершённый вид) and perfective (совершённый вид). Aspect shows whether an action is completed, ongoing, or repeated.
This is one of the most important concepts in Russian grammar! Understanding aspect is essential for using verbs correctly in any tense.

The Two Aspects

1. Imperfective Aspect (Несовершённый вид)

Emphasizes the process, duration, or repetition of an action. Answers "What was happening?" or "What do/did/will you do?"

я читаю книгу (I am reading a book - the process is ongoing)
я читал книгу (I was reading a book - I was in the process)
я буду читать книгу (I will be reading a book - the process will be ongoing)

2. Perfective Aspect (Совершённый вид)

Emphasizes completion, result, or a single completed action. Answers "What happened?" or "What did you do/will you do?"

я прочитаю книгу (I will read/finish the book - focus on completion)
я прочитал книгу (I read the book / I finished reading the book)
я вижу, что ты прочитал книгу (I see that you have read the book - action is complete)

Key Differences at a Glance

Aspect Focus English Translation Example
Imperfective Process, duration, habit to read, was reading, will read читать (to read)
Perfective Completion, result, one-time action to finish reading, read, will have read прочитать (to finish reading)

Forming Perfective from Imperfective

Most perfective verbs are formed by adding a prefix to the imperfective:

Prefix Meaning/Use Imperfective → Perfective
про- through, completion читать → прочитать
с- completion делать → сделать
по- beginning, limited duration идти → пойти
при- arrival ходить → приходить
вы- completion, exit писать → выписать
за- beginning спать → заспать

Aspect in Different Tenses

Present Tense

Imperfective only!
Я читаю книгу. (I am reading a book.)
Он говорит по-русски. (He speaks Russian.)
[Perfective has no present tense in standard usage]

Past Tense

Imperfective: Я читал книгу 3 часа. (I was reading the book for 3 hours - process)
Perfective: Я прочитал книгу за 3 часа. (I finished the book in 3 hours - result)

Future Tense

Imperfective: Я буду читать завтра. (I will be reading tomorrow - process)
Perfective: Я прочитаю завтра. (I will finish reading tomorrow - completion)

Common Aspect Pairs

Imperfective Perfective English
читать прочитать to read
писать написать to write
делать сделать to do/make
учить выучить to learn
говорить сказать to speak/say
покупать купить to buy
идти пойти to go on foot

When to Use Each Aspect

Use Imperfective For:

  • Ongoing, incomplete actions
  • Habitual or repeated actions
  • Describing a process
  • General facts (always imperfective in present)
  • Duration: "I was reading for 3 hours"

Use Perfective For:

  • Completed actions with a result
  • One-time actions (sequence of events)
  • Achieved goals or outcomes
  • Quick or sudden actions
  • Time frame: "I finished in 3 hours"

Practice Tips

  • Memorize common pairs: Get to know imperfective/perfective pairs together
  • Learn prefixes: Most perfectives use prefixes like про-, с-, по-, при-
  • Remember present tense: Always imperfective
  • Ask yourself: Is the action complete or ongoing?
  • Use context: Time expressions help: "в течение" (during) = imperfective, "за" (in, within) = perfective
