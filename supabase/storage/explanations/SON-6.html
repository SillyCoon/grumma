Prepositional Case - Complete Explanation

The prepositional case (предложный падеж), also called the locative case, answers О ком? (About whom?) and О чём? (About what?) or Где? (Where?). It always appears with a preposition and indicates location, topic, or the object of thought.
Important: The prepositional case NEVER appears without a preposition. It's the only case with this requirement.

Primary Functions

1. Location (с preposition В - in)

Shows where something or someone is located.

Я живу в городе. (I live in a city.)
"городе" (city) → prepositional case after "в" (in)

2. Location (с preposition НА - on)

On or at a surface, event, or place.

Книга на столе. (The book is on the table.)
"столе" (table) → prepositional case after "на" (on)

3. Topic of Thought or Speech (с preposition О - about)

What someone is thinking about, speaking about, or discussing.

Мы говорим об университете. (We are talking about the university.)
"университете" (university) → prepositional case after "об" (about)

4. Time (в + prepositional)

Indicating when something happens during a specific time.

В этом месяце я очень занят. (In this month I am very busy.)
"месяце" (month) → prepositional case after "в"

5. Other Prepositions

Prepositions like при (at, with), по (along, after), can also require prepositional case.

При университете есть общежитие. (There is a dormitory at the university.)
"университете" (university) → prepositional after "при"

Singular Endings

  • Masculine: -е, -и (стол → столе, день → дне)
  • Feminine: -е, -и (книга → книге, земля → земле)
  • Neuter: -е, -и (окно → окне, море → море)

Spelling Rules for Prepositional Endings

• After ж, ш, ч, щ, ц: use -е (but soft ending sounds like -i)
• After г, к, х: use -е (following 7-letter rule)
• After soft stems: use -и instead of -е
• Most other nouns: use -е

Common Prepositional Prepositions

в (в, во) in в доме (in a house)
на on, at на столе (on the table), на концерте (at a concert)
о (об, обо) about о друге (about a friend)
при at, with при университете (at the university)
по along, about по улице (along the street)

V vs. НА - Location Distinction

В is used for being inside something:
"в доме" (in the house), "в комнате" (in the room), "в Москве" (in Moscow)

На is used for being on a surface or at an event:
"на столе" (on the table), "на концерте" (at a concert), "на пляже" (on the beach)

Practice Tips

  • Remember: prepositional ALWAYS needs a preposition
  • Use "в" for locations inside; "на" for surfaces and events
  • Use "о" when discussing what you're talking/thinking about
  • Learn the prepositions first - they help identify prepositional
  • Most common: в, на, о are the ones you'll use most often
