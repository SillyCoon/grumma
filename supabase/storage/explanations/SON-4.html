Accusative Case - Complete Explanation

The accusative case (винительный падеж) answers Кого? (Whom?) and Что? (What?). It indicates the direct object - the person or thing directly affected by the action.

Primary Functions

1. Direct Object

Shows what or whom the action is performed on.

Я читаю книгу. (I read a book.)
"книгу" (book) → accusative case (direct object)

2. Time Expressions

Duration and specific time expressions often use the accusative.

Я учился весь день. (I studied the whole day.)
"весь день" (whole day) → accusative for duration

3. After Certain Prepositions

Prepositions like в (into), на (onto), про (about), через (through) can require the accusative.

Я пошёл в кино. (I went to the cinema.)
"кино" (cinema) → accusative after "в" (expressing motion)

The Animacy Distinction - IMPORTANT!

The accusative case has a special rule:

• INANIMATE nouns use the NOMINATIVE form in accusative
• ANIMATE nouns use the GENITIVE form in accusative

Inanimate Accusative (= Nominative)

Я вижу стол. (I see a table.)
"стол" stays the same (nominative = accusative for inanimate)

Я читаю книгу. (I read a book.)
"книгу" (accusative form for inanimate feminine)

Animate Accusative (= Genitive)

Я вижу кота. (I see a cat.)
"кота" (uses genitive ending for animate masculine)

Она любит своего брата. (She loves her brother.)
"брата" (uses genitive form for animate)

Accusative Endings Summary

Inanimate (=Nominative form):

  • Masculine: consonant (стол → стол)
  • Feminine: -а, -я (книга → книгу changes, but follows inanimate pattern)
  • Neuter: -о, -е (окно → окно)

Animate (=Genitive form):

  • Masculine: -а (кот → кота)
  • Feminine: -у (девушка → девушку)
  • Neuter: -а (животное → животное)

Common Accusative Verbs

видеть (to see) • читать (to read) • писать (to write) • любить (to love)
слышать (to hear) • брать (to take) • делать (to do) • проверять (to check)

Practice Tips

  • REMEMBER the animacy rule! This is crucial for Russian
  • Ask "what/whom is the action affecting?" to find direct objects
  • Inanimate objects look like nominative in singular
  • Animate objects look like genitive in singular
  • Common accusative = genitive for animate makes plural accusative = genitive too
