Past Simple Tense - Complete Explanation

The past simple tense (прошедшее простое время) expresses actions or states that occurred at a definite time in the past and are now completed. Russian has one simple past form, with gender and number variations.

Basic Function

The past tense describes:

  • Completed actions in the past
  • Events that happened at a specific time
  • Past states or conditions
  • Series of past events

Formation

Russian past tense is formed by removing the infinitive ending and adding gender/number suffixes:

читать (to read) → чита-
я читал (masculine - I read)
я читала (feminine - I read)
я читало (neuter - it read)
мы читали (plural - we read) [same for all genders]

Gender and Number Distinctions

Example with "быть" (to be)
Masculine singular: он был (he was)
Feminine singular: она была (she was)
Neuter singular: оно было (it was)
Plural (all genders): они были (they were)

Usage Examples

Completed Action

Я читал эту книгу вчера. (I read that book yesterday.)
Она написала письмо. (She wrote a letter.)

Past State

Он был в Москве. (He was in Moscow.)
Мы были рады. (We were happy.)

Series of Past Events

Я пришёл домой, позвонил другу, потом уснул.
(I came home, called a friend, then fell asleep.)

Aspect in Past Tense

The choice between imperfective and perfective verbs determines the meaning:

Imperfective (process/habit): Я читал книгу. (I was reading a book / I used to read books)
Perfective (completion): Я прочитал книгу. (I finished reading the book / I read the book)

Irregular Past Forms

быть: я был, ты был, он был, она была, оно было, они были
идти: я шёл, ты шёл, он шёл, она шла, оно шло, они шли
ехать: я ехал, ты ехал, он ехал, она ехала, они ехали

Important Notes

  • Past tense in Russian has gender and number agreement
  • In plural, all genders look the same (-и or -и)
  • Feminine ending is always -а regardless of verb type
  • Neuter ending is usually -о
  • Aspect choice affects the meaning significantly in past tense

Practice Tips

  • Remember gender differences, especially for feminine (-а)
  • Identify whether the verb is imperfective or perfective
  • Learn that plural past always ends in -и/-и
  • Pay attention to stress patterns which can shift
  • Practice recognizing both regular and irregular past forms
