Nominative Case - Complete Explanation

The nominative case (именительный падеж) is the basic, dictionary form of Russian nouns. It answers the questions Кто? (Who?) and Что? (What?)

Primary Functions

1. Subject of the Sentence

The nominative case identifies who or what is performing the action in a sentence.

Студент читает книгу. (The student reads a book.)
"студент" (student) is the subject → nominative case

2. Predicate Nominative

After the verb "быть" (to be) and similar linking verbs, the predicate noun takes the nominative case.

Я учитель. (I am a teacher.)
"учитель" (teacher) is the predicate nominative → nominative case

3. Apposition

When renaming or further identifying a noun, both nouns are in the nominative.

Москва, столица России, очень красивая. (Moscow, the capital of Russia, is very beautiful.)
Both "Москва" and "столица" are in nominative case

Key Characteristics

  • The nominative is the base form you find in dictionaries
  • Most masculine nouns end in a consonant or -ь
  • Most feminine nouns end in -а or -я
  • Most neuter nouns end in -о or -е
  • Plural nominatives typically end in -ы, -и, or -а

Common Endings

Singular: стол (table), книга (book), окно (window)
Plural: столы (tables), книги (books), окна (windows)

Practice Tips

  • Start every sentence by identifying the subject (nominative)
  • Look for who or what is "doing" something in the sentence
  • Remember that nominative is always the base form in dictionaries
  • Pay attention to gender endings - they help identify nominative forms
