Dative Case - Complete Explanation

The dative case (дательный падеж) answers Кому? (To whom?) and Чему? (To what?). It primarily indicates the indirect object - the person or thing that benefits from or is affected by the action.

Primary Functions

1. Indirect Object

Shows to whom or for whom something is done.

Я дал книгу другу. (I gave the book to a friend.)
"другу" (friend) → dative case (indirect object)

2. Verbs of Giving and Helping

Verbs like дать (to give), давать (to give), помогать (to help), дарить (to gift) require the dative.

Она помогает своей матери. (She helps her mother.)
"матери" (mother) → dative with "помогает"

3. Impersonal Constructions with "нравиться"

The person who likes something is in the dative case.

Мне нравится эта книга. (I like this book - literally: This book pleases me.)
"мне" (me) → dative with "нравиться"

4. After Certain Prepositions

Prepositions like к (toward), по (along), благодаря (thanks to) require the dative.

Я пошёл к друзьям. (I went to my friends.)
"друзьям" (friends) → dative after "к"

5. Age and Correspondence

With expressions of age: "Ему 25 лет" (He is 25 years old).

Ему 20 лет. (He is 20 years old.)
"ему" (him) → dative with age

Singular Endings

  • Masculine: -у, -ю (стол → столу, день → дню)
  • Feminine: -е, -е (книга → книге, земля → земле)
  • Neuter: -у, -ю (окно → окну, море → морю)

Common Dative Verbs

давать/дать (to give) • дарить (to gift) • помогать (to help) • писать (to write to)
звонить (to call) • рассказывать (to tell) • показывать (to show) • нравиться (to like)

Practice Tips

  • Think of dative as answering "to whom" questions
  • Remember "мне нравится" pattern for preferences
  • Look for verbs of giving, sending, helping
  • Note the distinct dative endings (different from genitive)
  • Dative is often used for the recipient or beneficiary of an action
