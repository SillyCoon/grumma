Genitive Case - Complete Explanation

The genitive case (родительный падеж) is the most frequently used case after the nominative. It answers Кого? (Whom?) and Чего? (What?). It primarily expresses possession, origin, and relationships.

Primary Functions

1. Possession and Relationship

Shows that something belongs to or is related to someone/something.

Это дом студента. (This is the student's house.)
"студента" (student) → genitive case shows possession

2. Negation

With the word "нет" (there is no), nouns appear in the genitive case.

У меня нет ручки. (I don't have a pen.)
"ручки" (pen) → genitive with negation

3. After Prepositions

Many prepositions require the genitive case: из (from), без (without), около (near), для (for), вместо (instead of), во время (during).

Она пришла из университета. (She came from the university.)
"университета" (university) → genitive after preposition "из"

4. Material and Origin

Describing what something is made of or where it comes from.

Это дом из дерева. (This is a wooden house - a house made of wood.)
"дерева" (wood) → genitive showing material

5. Quantity and Amount

Numbers and expressions of quantity often use the genitive.

Пять студентов приехали. (Five students arrived.)
"студентов" (students) → genitive with number 5

Singular Endings

  • Masculine: -а, -я (стол → стола)
  • Feminine: -ы, -и (книга → книги, земля → земли)
  • Neuter: -а, -я (окно → окна, море → моря)

Common Genitive Expressions

У меня есть... (I have...) - the object is in genitive
Не было... (There wasn't...) - the object is in genitive
Много/мало... (Much/little...) - followed by genitive

Practice Tips

  • Look for possession: use genitive for "of" in English
  • Watch for "нет" - always followed by genitive
  • Remember key prepositions that require genitive
  • Note that genitive endings differ significantly from nominative
  • Practice recognizing genitive forms by their endings
