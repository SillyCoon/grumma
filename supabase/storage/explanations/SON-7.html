Present Simple Tense - Complete Explanation

The present simple tense (настоящее простое время) in Russian expresses actions that are happening now, habitual actions, or general truths. It is formed from imperfective verbs only.

Basic Function

The present tense describes:

  • Actions happening right now
  • Habitual or repeated actions
  • General truths or facts
  • Ongoing states or conditions

Formation

Russian present tense verbs conjugate by person and number. There are two main conjugation patterns:

First Conjugation (-ать, -ять verbs)

читать (to read)
я читаю (I read)
ты читаешь (you read)
он/она/оно читает (he/she/it reads)
мы читаем (we read)
вы читаете (you read - formal/plural)
они читают (they read)

Second Conjugation (-ить verbs)

говорить (to speak)
я говорю (I speak)
ты говоришь (you speak)
он/она/оно говорит (he/she/it speaks)
мы говорим (we speak)
вы говорите (you speak - formal/plural)
они говорят (they speak)

Usage Examples

Right Now

Он сейчас читает газету. (He is reading a newspaper right now.)
Что ты делаешь? (What are you doing?)

Habitual Action

Каждый день я хожу в университет. (Every day I go to the university.)
Она работает учителем. (She works as a teacher.)

General Truth

Вода кипит при 100 градусах. (Water boils at 100 degrees.)
Солнце светит днём. (The sun shines during the day.)

Irregular Verbs

быть (to be) - я есть (archaic) / я (present implied)
идти (to go - on foot) - я иду, ты идёшь, он идёт...
ехать (to go - by vehicle) - я еду, ты едешь, он едет...

Important Notes

  • Only imperfective verbs are used for present tense in Russian
  • Perfective verbs have no present tense (they focus on completion)
  • Russian present tense can mean "is reading", "reads", or "am reading" depending on context
  • Stress patterns can shift in conjugation

Practice Tips

  • Identify the verb's conjugation type (1st or 2nd)
  • Learn common present tense endings by heart
  • Note irregular verbs like идти and ехать
  • Remember to use imperfective verbs for ongoing or habitual actions
  • Practice conjugating verbs you encounter
